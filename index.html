Columbia University Orchestra

Founded by composer Edward MacDowell in 1896, the Columbia University Orchestra is the oldest continually operating university orchestra in the United States.

Support & Join

About CUO

The principal mission of the Orchestra is to expose talented student musicians to the highest level of orchestral repertoire. We offer rigorous coursework, regular rehearsals, and seasonal concerts at premier venues—including our Spring Concert at Alice Tully Hall—fostering a high-caliber musical environment at Columbia University.

Explore the Orchestra

People & Member's Area

Our ensemble consists of passionate undergraduate and graduate musicians. Enrolled members have access to exclusive rehearsal schedules, sheet music, and internal resources.

Events & Rehearsals

Operating as a formal course, the orchestra holds weekly rehearsals culminating in highly anticipated public concerts and events throughout the academic year.

Audio & Gallery

Explore our legacy through our extensive gallery and listen to audio recordings of past performances, capturing the dedication of our soloists and maestros.
